Question 1 of 10

1. A rectangular public pool is 75 feet long and 30 feet wide. The shallow end is 3 feet deep, and the deep end drops to 9 feet deep. What is the total volume of the pool in gallons?

2. A rectangular community pool is 60 feet long and 20 feet wide with a constant depth of 4 feet. What is the total volume of this pool in gallons?

3. A rectangular resort lap pool measures 100 feet long by 15 feet wide. The shallow end starts at 4 feet and slants down to a maximum depth of 6 feet. What is the total water volume?

4. Your pool volume is 40,000 gallons. Your current Total Alkalinity reading is 60 ppm, and your target is 90 ppm. How many pounds of Sodium Bicarbonate do you need to add?

5. A 25,000-gallon pool requires a change from 0 ppm Free Chlorine to 4 ppm Free Chlorine. How many total ounces of Calcium Hypochlorite (67%) are required?

6. You manage a large 80,000-gallon pool. You need to raise free chlorine from 1 ppm to 4 ppm using Calcium Hypochlorite (67%). How many total ounces do you need, and what is that weight converted into pounds?

7. A 30,000-gallon pool has a Total Chlorine (TC) of 3.5 ppm and a Free Chlorine (FC) of 2.5 ppm. How much Sodium Hypochlorite (12%) do we need to add to reach Breakpoint Chlorination? (Convert to Gallons).

8. Calculate the Saturation Index (SI) for the following pool: pH 7.4, Temp 84°F, Calcium Hardness 400 ppm, Total Alkalinity 100 ppm, and TDS 1000 ppm.

9. Calculate the Saturation Index (SI) for a pool with: pH 7.2, Temp 60°F, Calcium Hardness 150 ppm, Total Alkalinity 80 ppm, and TDS 750. What is the saturation index?

10. A 60,000-gallon pool has a Total Chlorine reading of 2.3 ppm and a Free Chlorine reading of 1.5 ppm. Calculate how many pounds of Calcium Hypochlorite (67%) are needed to achieve breakpoint chlorination.
